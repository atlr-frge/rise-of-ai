## The name

**Materiality.**

In securities law and accounting, materiality is the formal threshold at which a piece of information becomes important enough to change a decision-maker's judgment. It predates the ESG version of the word by decades. That definition is the entire thesis of the series in one term: the machine can surface everything, but deciding what clears the bar, what is *material* right now, is a human act.

We lead with the original, markets meaning. Not the ESG checklist. The securities one: *important enough to change the decision.*

## Why it replaces "Rise of AI"

"Rise of AI" makes the machine the hero. Everything we actually sell says the opposite: the human judgment call is the edge. Materiality fixes that in one word, and it does three things "Rise of AI" never could:

1. It names the exact mechanism from Dan Shipper's *After Automation*: AI floods the world with competence, and the scarce human act is framing what matters.
2. It speaks the audience's native language. Allocators, PMs, and advisors know materiality cold. It signals seriousness rather than hype.
3. It is effectively unclaimed in finance media. No markets podcast, newsletter, or fund owns it.

## The thesis spine (from *After Automation*, Dan Shipper, Every)

- **Slop** is visible sameness, the default when everyone runs the same tools. A market full of slop pays whoever can tell what is material from what is noise.
- **The frame vs. the framer.** Models climb the frame humans set. They never catch the human deciding what matters in real time. That decision is a materiality judgment.
- **The Human Sandwich.** Humans frame, AI executes, humans evaluate. This is both our editorial process and the fund's governance.
- **The more we automate, the more expert human work there is to do.** Lead with this optimism.

## The ETF tie (this is the strongest part)

Materiality is not a metaphor for what the AI Alpha ETF does. It *is* what it does. The fund's engine "turns messy language into structured, auditable evidence," and the human PM decides what is material enough to move the book. The series name is the methodology, said in public.

- **Pairing line:** *AI Alpha finds the alpha. Materiality is how.*
- **Thematic pair:** the fund decides what's material. The series shows the work.
- **Bonus:** "material information" is securities language, which reads as credible and serious to the exact people the fund raises from.

## Brand architecture, Atelier publishes, AI Alpha is the disclosed thesis

*Decided 2026-06-02. This governs every external piece.*

**Atelier Superintelligence is the publisher. AI Alpha is the disclosed thesis Materiality
explains. Lead with Atelier; reference AI Alpha.** Neither brand "takes over", they sit in a
fixed endorsed relationship.

**Why this structure:**
1. **Credibility.** "An Atelier briefing" reads as independent analysis; people share insight,
   not ads. The flows thesis depends on it feeling like education, not promotion.
2. **Compliance.** AI Alpha is a regulated vehicle. Content under the *fund's* brand becomes
   fund marketing (performance-claim limits, mandatory disclaimers, likely pre-clearance).
   Atelier-published editorial that *discloses* affiliation is the lighter, safer posture,
   and cleaner given the Canaccord/Al sensitivity.
3. **Durable asset.** The Atelier brand compounds and is ours; Materiality survives any change
   to the fund's structure.

### The rules
- **Publisher / endorsement (always on):** `MATERIALITY · an Atelier Superintelligence
  briefing`. Atelier is the named publisher on the wordmark lockup, end cards, profiles, bios.
- **AI Alpha = disclosed affiliation, never the publisher.** Reference it as the *thesis*,
  not the author. No AI Alpha logo headlining the content; AI Alpha is not shown as publisher.
- **On-camera:** no AI Alpha, Canaccord, or Al Verma as an on-screen co-brand (already
  decided). Michael fronts as Atelier.

### Credit-line + disclaimer pattern (copy)
- **End card:** `MATERIALITY` · `an Atelier Superintelligence briefing` · *(smaller)* `The
  public thesis behind the AI Alpha ETF.`
- **Footer / caption disclaimer (with any AI Alpha reference or market verdict):** *"Editorial
  opinion, not investment advice. Not an offer or solicitation to buy any security.
  [Compliance-cleared through Canaccord Genuity.]"*

### Caveat, compliance owns the final wording
The boundary between "disclosed affiliation" and regulated fund marketing is a **regulatory**
call, not a branding one. The exact AI Alpha credit line and disclaimer above must be approved
by compliance / Canaccord before publish. This doc sets the *structure*; compliance sets the
*language*.

## Positioning (one paragraph)

Materiality is a market briefing for people who allocate capital in the AI transition. Each episode takes one structural constraint, power, agents, labor, reshoring, trust, and makes the case for whether it is material: important enough to change how you position. It is the public thesis behind the AI Alpha ETF, that as competence gets cheap, the entire game becomes deciding what matters.
