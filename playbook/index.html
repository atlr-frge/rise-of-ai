Context

01The series, in one screen

What it is

Materiality is a short-form markets briefing for the people who allocate capital in the AI transition — allocators, advisors, sophisticated investors. Each piece takes one structural force and says whether it's material (important enough to change how you're positioned) or noise.

Brand architecture

Atelier Superintelligence publishes it; AI Alpha is the disclosed thesis. Michael fronts it, on camera, for real. Logo = the MATERIALITY wordmark.

Domains secured: materiality.ca · materiality.pro

Why production quality is the whole game

The thesis, in the deck's own words: no startup out-researches Goldman on engine alone — the moat that compounds is content.

So the content can't look like AI slop. It has to look cinematic, intentional, and unmistakably ours. This playbook is the system that makes that repeatable.

The core method

02The one move that makes it cohesive

The difference between a brand and AI slop is cohesion — every frame looking like one intentional world. We get that from a single discipline:

One locked reference image.
We approve one hero still — the Materiality style and color grade — and feed it into every single generation. The first approved image becomes the reference for all the others, so lighting, color, and texture hold across the whole piece. That one rule is what makes a series read as a single brand. It's the engine behind "recognizable throughout."

The method

04The pipeline, end to end

Script

Develop angles, draft, refine by voice. Hooks are everything.

Film the talking head first real Michael

Multiple takes, natural delivery. Then we design the visuals around it.

Storyboard with Claude

Direction plus what we won't do (no stock B-roll). A tactile concept → color schemes + prompts.

Lock the look

Source the grade from real cinema; set it as the reference image.

Still first — then lock it Nano Banana Pro

Land the hero still, then make it the reference for everything. Control lives in the still.

Animate Higgsfield · Kling 3.0 · multi-shot

Subtle, intentional camera motion. Keep the best 1–2 seconds of each.

Chain & cut

End-frame chaining for builds; assemble to a tight, scroll-stopping pace.

The craft principles

05What keeps it premium

Reference-image lock
One hero still in every prompt; roll the first approved image forward. The cohesion engine.
Still-first
Get the image right, then animate. Never text-to-video.
Subtle motion default
subtle camera motion + Kling 3.0 + multi-shot; subtle subject motion for people.
End-frame chaining
For builds where elements accumulate, the end frame becomes the next start frame — seamless continuity.
Tactile, never stock
A real, hand-built, analyst's-desk world. The opposite of generic AI footage.
