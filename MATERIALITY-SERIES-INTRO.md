## What we're actually copying (from the real clip)

**Her script skeleton (transcribed):**
1. Cold numbered hook, no intro: *"There are five stages to AI, and most people only know
   of level one."*
2. **Demote the obvious:** *"Most people think this is AI, but in reality, this is just the
   foundation."*
3. **Climb the ladder**: each stage escalates ("where it gets interesting" → "the big one"
   → "the final stage"), each with ONE vivid concrete example, and she locates the viewer:
   *"this is where we are right now."*
4. **End on a big binary stakes question:** *"the best thing that's ever happened to
   humankind, or the most destructive?"*
5. **Callback close:** *"There are five stages."*

**Her visual system (from the frames):**
- Warm talking-head, natural light, **podcast mic in frame**, animated hands, direct to lens.
- **Top-down handcrafted b-roll:** a hand sketching a pyramid in a notebook *live*, building it
  layer by layer with **physical sticky-note labels**, plus tactile paper craft (a DNA helix
  built from sticky notes) and **real-prop metaphors** on the desk (an owl figurine + neon).
- Clean **word-by-word kinetic captions**, centered, white, with a small play-triangle motif.

**Our translation:** same skeleton, same warmth, same handcrafted tactile world, but the
pyramid she draws becomes **our pyramid of forces, marked up with the ember marker (The
Mark)**, and the demoted "level one" becomes the model hype everyone watches. The payoff
flips from "utopia or doom" to **"the only edge left is deciding what's material."**

## Production notes
- **One Michael shoot** covers the trailer's talking-head AND every episode's cold-open/sign-off.
  Mic in frame, warm natural light, direct to lens, match Emily's intimacy, not a news desk.
- **The pyramid is the hero prop.** Build it for real on paper, shoot top-down, add a sticky-note
  layer per force. This single sequence becomes the reusable visual that ties the series together.
- The five document plates marked here become the **reusable Mark library** for the episodes.
- **Captions:** word-by-word, centered, heavy weight, punch word flips ember (see system §6).
- **Compliance:** materiality verdicts are editorial opinion on mispricing, not advice. Standard
  disclaimer on the end card, Canaccord-cleared.

## Second format spotted (bank for episodes)

Her clip also contained a **single-story curiosity piece**: *"AI models just got caught
sharing secret messages, but nobody can figure out how"* (the owl / subliminal-learning
study): hook → walk through the experiment → the unexplained twist → a "we can't see, can't
filter, don't understand it" payoff. **This is the perfect template for individual episodes:**
one fascinating, true, well-sourced story → followed to the materiality verdict. Worth a
dedicated episode-format doc if we like it.
