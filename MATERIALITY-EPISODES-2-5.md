## Episode 2 - The Agent Moment
**Force:** agents and production automation. **Verdict:** the agent *tool* is noise; redrawing
the *workflow* around it is material.

**1 · HOOK (avatar, tight, direct):**
> "An AI agent just opened a support ticket, handed it to a second agent, got the work done,
> and closed it. No human touched any of it. By the end of this year, that stops being a demo.
> It becomes how roughly 40 percent of enterprise software actually runs." `[verify 40%]`

**2 · WALK (b-roll: server aisle, terminal running tasks → product doc):**
> "Here is what changed. AI stopped answering questions and started doing the work. You don't
> ask it, you assign it. And it is not one agent. It is twenty, handing off to each other,
> one researching, one writing, one executing, with no person in the loop."
> → **Circle** "embedded agents." Scrawl: `the real shift`. Tag: `enterprise apps, 2026`.

**3 · TURN (b-roll: org chart, dashboards):**
> "Most companies are buying the agent and bolting it onto the old process. That is the
> mistake. The value was never the tool. It was tearing up the workflow the tool now runs."
> → **Strike** the word "tool." Scrawl: `not the point`.

**4 · NAME (Constraint Card):**
> `WE STOPPED BUILDING SOFTWARE HUMANS USE. WE STARTED BUILDING SOFTWARE THAT USES SOFTWARE.`

**5 · VERDICT:**
> "So the call is simple. The agent you can buy off a shelf, every competitor has it. That is
> noise. The company that redesigns its whole operation around agents, that is the one that
> compounds. The workflow is material."
> → `THE WORKFLOW` Circled (MATERIAL) · `THE TOOL` Struck (NOISE).

**6 · STACK:** "How this was made: my ElevenLabs voice, a HeyGen avatar, Seedance b-roll, marked

about fifteen dollars. What broke: the agent over-wrote the captions, so I cut them by hand." `[real numbers]`

## Episode 3 - The Labor Reckoning
**Force:** labor and the reallocation of judgment. **Verdict:** the headcount number is noise;
where the judgment goes is material.

**1 · HOOK (avatar, serious, no smile):**
> "A major tech CEO told his staff the layoffs were not because AI replaced them. They were
> because AI *might*. Sit with that. The cuts are arriving before the capability does." `[verify CEO attribution]`

**2 · WALK (b-roll: empty desks, layoff headlines → labor data):**
> "Through the first half of this year, tens of thousands of tech roles were cut with AI named
> as the reason. Worker fear of job loss jumped from 28 to 40 percent. The IMF called the
> coming shift a tsunami."
> → **Circle** the share who now fear displacement. Scrawl: `the real number`. Tag: `labor data, 2026`. `[verify all figures]`

**3 · TURN (b-roll: a busy team, then one analyst):**
> "But here is the part the doom headlines miss. The same period saw over a million new
> AI-related roles created. Work is not vanishing. It is being repriced. The routine part is
> going to zero, and the judgment part is going to the moon."
> → **Strike** "headcount." Scrawl: `wrong metric`. `[verify 1M+]`

**4 · NAME (Constraint Card):**
> `AI ISN'T COMING FOR THE JOB. IT'S COMING FOR THE BUSINESS MODEL BENEATH IT.`

**5 · VERDICT:**
> "So watching the layoff count tells you almost nothing. That is noise. Watching where the
> judgment moves, who gets paid to decide rather than to produce, that tells you everything.

> → `WHERE JUDGMENT GOES` Circled (MATERIAL) · `THE HEADCOUNT` Struck (NOISE).

## Episode 4 - Atoms Are Fashionable Again
**Force:** capital rotation into hard assets. **Verdict:** the software multiple is noise; the
physical bottleneck you can own is material. *(Differentiator vs. Ep 1: Ep 1 is the physical
limit, you cannot build power fast enough. Ep 4 is the capital flow, money fleeing software
into the things that limit gets priced into.)*

**1 · HOOK (avatar, confident):**
> "The best-performing AI trade this year is not a chip company and it is not a model lab. It
> is a nuclear utility. While the software crowd argues about multiples, the money quietly
> went and bought the power plant." `[verify Constellation / utility outperformance]`

**2 · WALK (b-roll: reactors, ports, fabs → capex chart):**
> "For twenty years software ate the world. Now AI is eating software, and the capital is
> rushing back into things you can touch. Roughly 660 to 690 billion in big-tech capex this
> year, and a chunk of it is concrete, copper, and uranium."
> → **Circle** the capex line. Scrawl: `follow this`. Tag: `2026 capex`. `[verify $660-690B]`

**3 · TURN (b-roll: a software office, then a substation):**
> "The reflex is to keep paying up for the asset-light software names. That is the crowded
> trade. The contrarian one is the boring physical bottleneck that every AI build now depends
> on, and that nobody can spin up overnight."
> → **Strike** "asset-light." Scrawl: `priced in`.

**4 · NAME (Constraint Card):**
> `THE SMART MONEY QUIETLY LEFT THE CLOUD. IT'S BUYING THE POWER PLANT.`

**5 · VERDICT:**
> "So the call. A rich software multiple in a commoditizing market, that is noise. A physical
> chokepoint you can actually own, power, cooling, the grid, the fuel, that is material."
> → `THE PHYSICAL BOTTLENECK` Circled (MATERIAL) · `THE SOFTWARE MULTIPLE` Struck (NOISE).

**6 · STACK:** "How this was made: my voice, a HeyGen avatar, Seedance for the infrastructure
b-roll, marked up by hand. Cost, about fifteen dollars. What broke: Seedance kept making the
reactor glow neon, so I regraded it to near-black." `[real numbers]`

## Episode 5 - The Verification Crisis
**Force:** trust, liability, and verification. **Verdict:** the model's fluency is noise;
provable truth is becoming the material asset. *(Recommended: shoot this one with real Michael,
not the avatar - a synthetic narrator delivering the verification episode is the one ironic case.)*

**1 · HOOK (real Michael or avatar, urgent):**
> "This August, in Europe, deploying certain AI you cannot prove is safe becomes illegal. And a
> Stanford team just found that the logging the law requires has no tamper-proof mechanism. The
> rule shows up before the tool to obey it does." `[verify Stanford finding; date is Aug 2, 2026]`

**2 · WALK (b-roll: regulatory chamber, signatures → a filing):**
> "Here is the squeeze. AI can now generate anything, and almost nobody can prove it got it
> right. Only about 6 percent of engineers say they fully trust AI output. Detected deepfake
> cases jumped roughly nine-fold in a year. Trust is becoming the scarce input."
> → **Circle** "cannot prove." Scrawl: `the asset now`. Tag: `EU AI Act, Aug 2 2026`. `[verify 6%, 9x]`

**3 · TURN (b-roll: corporate filings):**
> "And it is already in the disclosures. Nearly three-quarters of the largest US companies have
> warned investors about *material* AI risk. They are using the word in the securities sense,
> the exact bar this whole show is built on."
> → **Strike** "move fast." Scrawl: `over`. `[verify 72% S&P 500]`

**4 · NAME (Constraint Card):**
> `WHEN YOU CAN'T TELL WHAT'S REAL, THE LAW DECIDES WHAT COUNTS.`

**5 · VERDICT:**
> "So the call. A model that sounds confident and fluent, everyone has that, it is noise. The
> ability to *prove* an output is real, sourced, and auditable, that is becoming the most
> valuable thing in the stack. Provable truth is material."
> → `PROVABLE TRUTH` Circled (MATERIAL) · `FLUENCY` Struck (NOISE).

**6 · STACK:** "How this was made, and this one matters: my real voice, sourced filings, every
